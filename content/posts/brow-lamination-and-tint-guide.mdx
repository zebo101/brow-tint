Brow lamination and tint is the treatment that took the "Instagram brow" mainstream — the brushed-up, fluffy, sculpted look that makes thin or unruly brows read as full and intentional. It combines two distinct salon procedures into one appointment: a chemical step that reshapes the brow hairs upward, and a tinting step that adds color depth.

This guide covers what actually happens during the appointment, how brow lamination and tint differs from lash and brow tint (a different combined treatment people often confuse it with), realistic longevity, aftercare, cost ranges, and how to see the result on your own photo before you commit to two hours in a salon chair.

## What brow lamination and tint actually is

Brow lamination is, mechanically, a perm for your eyebrows. The brow artist applies a softening cream that breaks the disulfide bonds in your brow hairs, brushes the hairs into the desired direction (typically straight up and outward for the "feathered" look), then applies a neutralizing solution that locks the new shape in place.

The tint is applied separately — usually after lamination, while the hairs are still in their new position. A semi-permanent dye colors the brow hairs and lightly stains the skin underneath, adding the color depth that makes the now-brushed-up brows read as full instead of wispy.

The two steps together produce the "soap brow" finish without daily soap-brow effort. The combined result lasts longer than the tint alone because the lamination keeps the brow hairs in a position where their full color shows.

## How the appointment actually goes

A typical brow lamination and tint appointment runs **45–75 minutes** and follows this flow:

1. **Consultation and shape decision.** The brow artist looks at your natural brow growth, discusses how brushed-up vs softly lifted you want the result, and confirms a tint shade.
2. **Cleansing.** Brows and surrounding skin are cleaned to remove makeup and oils.
3. **Step 1 — softening cream.** Applied for 8–15 minutes depending on your brow hair coarseness. This is the chemical step that breaks the bonds.
4. **Brushing into shape.** The artist brushes the now-pliable hairs into the agreed direction.
5. **Step 2 — neutralizer.** Applied for 8–15 minutes to lock the new shape.
6. **Tinting.** The semi-permanent dye is applied for 5–15 minutes depending on shade depth and your hair porosity.
7. **Cleanup and aftercare instructions.** Skin tint may be cleaned off the surrounding skin; you'll be told to keep brows dry for 24 hours.

You walk out with a result that looks slightly more dramatic than the four-week steady state — color settles and the lamination relaxes a little in the first week.

## Brow lamination vs brow tint vs the combination

These three terms get used interchangeably in casual conversation but mean different things:

- **Brow tint alone** — semi-permanent dye that colors the brow hairs and skin underneath. Doesn't change brow shape or direction. Lasts 4–6 weeks. ~15 minute appointment.
- **Brow lamination alone** — chemical reshaping of the brow hairs to grow upward and outward. Doesn't add color. Lasts 6–8 weeks. ~30–45 minute appointment.
- **Brow lamination AND tint** (combined) — both procedures back-to-back in one appointment. Maximum transformation. Lasts 4–6 weeks for the full effect. ~45–75 minute appointment.

Most salons offer the combined treatment as a discounted package vs booking the two services separately. The combined visual change is significantly bigger than either treatment alone — see [our brow tint before and after guide](/blog/brow-tint-before-and-after) for what to expect from each.

## Lash and brow tint — a different combined treatment

Lash and brow tint is a separate combined treatment people sometimes confuse with brow lamination and tint. The difference:

- **Lash and brow tint** = tinting the eyelashes + tinting the brows. No lamination on either. The eye-framing effect is from coordinated color depth, not shape change.
- **Brow lamination and tint** = lamination of brows + tint of brows only. Lashes untouched.

You can absolutely combine all three (lash tint + brow lamination + brow tint) in one extended appointment if you want the maximum eye-framing effect. Many salons price this as a "full eye package" or similar.

If you're undecided between lash and brow tint vs brow lamination and tint, the visual difference is significant: lash and brow tint gives you defined-without-effort framing; brow lamination and tint gives you a transformed brow shape. They solve different problems.

## How much does brow lamination and tint cost?

Pricing varies meaningfully by region and salon tier:

- **United States** — typically $80–$200 for the combined treatment
- **United Kingdom** — typically £55–£120
- **Australia** — typically AUD $80–$150
- **EU mainland** — typically €60–€130

Higher prices typically reflect: brand-name salon in a metro area, recently-trained brow artist with strong portfolio, or premium product brands like Brow Code or RefectoCil. The cheapest options are usually new brow artists building their portfolio — quality varies widely; ask to see recent work.

## Aftercare that actually matters

The first 24 hours determine how well the lamination holds:

- **Keep brows dry for 24 hours.** No water, no sweat, no steam (skip the gym sauna).
- **Don't touch or rub.** The hairs are setting in their new position; pressure undoes the work.
- **No makeup on the brows for 24 hours.** Skin underneath needs to seal.

Long-term care:
- **Brush brows daily** with a spoolie to maintain the lifted shape.
- **Apply a brow conditioning oil** (castor or specialized brow serum) 2–3x per week to prevent over-drying. Lamination is mildly damaging to the hair shaft; conditioning extends the healthy look.
- **Avoid oil-based cleansers on the brow area** for the first week — they accelerate tint fading.
- **Schedule the next appointment at 6 weeks** to maintain consistent results.

## Who shouldn't get brow lamination and tint

This treatment isn't for everyone. Skip it if:

- **You've had a reaction to brow tint or hair dye before.** The chemical lamination plus traditional tint multiplies the irritation potential. Henna brow tint with no lamination is a safer alternative.
- **Your brows are already very sparse.** Lamination doesn't add hair — it reshapes existing hair. Sparse brows post-lamination can look stretched and even sparser.
- **You're pregnant or nursing.** The chemicals haven't been studied enough; most salons decline service.
- **You have active eczema, dermatitis, or skin sensitivity around the brows.** Wait until skin is calm.
- **You wear glasses constantly.** Lamination's brushed-up direction sometimes conflicts with glasses' frame contact, and the fluffy look can read as messy under heavy frame coverage.

A consultation with the brow artist before booking is worth the 10 minutes — most will give honest advice on whether the treatment fits your brows and lifestyle.

## Quick FAQ

**Is brow lamination and tint permanent?** No. Lamination lasts 6–8 weeks; tint lasts 4–6 weeks; the combined visible result lasts about 4–6 weeks.

**Can I get brow lamination without the tint?** Yes — most salons offer it as a standalone service. The result will look brushed-up but won't have the color depth that makes lamination most flattering.

**Does it damage your brow hair?** Repeated lamination (every 6 weeks for years) can dry the hair shaft. Brow conditioning oil mitigates this. Most brow artists recommend a 1-month break every 6 months.

**How is this different from microblading?** Microblading deposits semi-permanent pigment into the skin (lasts 1–3 years, much more invasive). Brow lamination + tint affects the existing hair only.
